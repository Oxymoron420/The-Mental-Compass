How to Use the Mental Compass

This site is a tool, not a judge. It helps you notice where you are on the spectrum of:

  • Negative spirals ↔ Positive spirals
  • Fear/avoidance ↔ Courage/curiosity
  • Masking ↔ True self

Nothing here is a diagnosis. It's a mirror to help you see your own patterns with more compassion and clarity.

Negative vs Positive Spirals

A negative spiral pulls you down: overwhelm → shame → avoidance → more chaos.

A positive spiral pulls you up: curiosity → insight → small action → self-trust.

The Compass doesn't magically erase pain; it helps you make one different choice at the pivot point.

Three Modes of the Compass

  • Universal Mode: gentle, safe reflection for anyone.
  • Neurodivergent Mode: slower pacing, more grounding, anchor checks.
  • Your OS Mode: deep, dual-processing — this one is for you, not for the public.

This site uses the first two modes. Your advanced model lives in your head (and whatever you build next).

The Mental Keys & Their Opposites

These keys describe how your nervous system is leaning in each moment. They’re not moral judgments; they’re dials.

Self

Confidence
Conviction

When these are active, you feel “I am capable, I can act.” When suppressed, shame, self-doubt, and paralysis show up.

Safety

Trust
Love

Safety isn't just physical; it's emotional and relational. When these are blocked, hypervigilance and isolation appear.

Drive

Courage
Curiosity

This is your willingness to move toward the unknown. When blocked, fear, avoidance, and apathy take over.

Truth

Honesty
Compassion

Truth without compassion wounds. Compassion without honesty lies. Together, they unlock clear, grounded truth.

Opposites (for awareness, not self-blame):
  • Confidence ↔ shame / worthlessness
  • Conviction ↔ indecision / collapse
  • Courage ↔ fear / avoidance
  • Curiosity ↔ apathy / shut-down
  • Trust ↔ hypervigilance / suspicion
  • Love ↔ rejection sensitivity / withdrawal
  • Honesty ↔ denial / self-deception
  • Compassion ↔ self-hatred / cruelty
Spirals

Negative & Positive Spirals

A spiral is just what happens when you repeat the same direction a few times in a row. Downward or upward.

Negative Spiral (Downward)

  • Overstimulation or stress hits.
  • Emotion spikes: fear, anger, shame.
  • Masking increases; you hide how bad it feels.
  • Overwhelm → freeze, numb, or explode.
  • Memory gets patchy; you feel “broken.”
  • Belief forms: “I can’t trust myself.”

Positive Spiral (Upward)

  • Curiosity notices: “Something is happening in me.”
  • You ground first (body, breath, anchor, music).
  • You name what you feel, without judgment.
  • You take one small action that aligns with truth.
  • Self-trust rises: “I handled that a little better.”
  • The next time is easier to face.
Switching spirals is a choice you make repeatedly.
Not a single dramatic decision, but a series of small, compassionate corrections.
Deep Introspection Mode

Chasing Trains (Following Trains of Thought)

⚠️
Before you chase trains:
Please update your anchors and check your current stress level. Deep thinking can amplify whatever state you're already in.

“Chasing trains” = intentionally following a thought all the way through to see what it connects to. It can unlock insight, but it can also deepen spirals if you’re not grounded.

1. Check Your Anchors

Anchors are things that keep you from floating away:

People anchors (who grounds you?)
Activity anchors
Environment anchors
Choose at least one person, one activity, and one place that you can return to if the thoughts get too heavy.

2. Ground Before You Dive

Use at least one of these before you go deep:

  • Notice 3 things you can see, 3 you can feel, 3 you can hear.
  • Slow breathing: inhale 4, hold 2, exhale 6 (repeat 5–10 times).
  • Gentle stims: stretching, rocking, pacing, finger tapping, whistling.
  • Play a calm track (lo-fi, instrumental) while you think.

3. How to Chase a Train Safely

  • Pick one question to follow, not ten.
  • Write it down first.
  • If your stress goes above 7/10, pause and ground again.
  • If a memory feels sharp and painful, you can write “this is too much for now” and leave it for later.

Check Myself as a Ground

Use this when you want to see if you are a stable anchor for someone else or for yourself.
  • Have I eaten, hydrated, and slept enough?
  • Is my stress below 7/10?
  • Can I listen without making it about me?
  • Do I have the energy to hold someone else’s weight right now?
You can use the stress slider + a short journal entry to decide: “Am I grounded enough to help, or do I need support too?”

Reality Check

Use this when thoughts feel heavy or absolute.
  • Is this a fact, a fear, or a story I'm telling myself?
  • What would I tell a friend who said this about themselves?
  • Is there at least one alternative explanation?
  • Am I tired, hungry, or overwhelmed right now?
You can write your thought, then answer these questions in your own words.
